What Is Pediatric Preventive Dentistry?

Pediatric preventive dentistry focuses on avoiding dental issues before they become painful or expensive to treat. For children, this means early intervention, regular checkups, and personalized care that supports the natural development of their teeth and jaws.

  • Dental Exams & Cleanings: Gentle cleanings every six months to remove plaque and tartar.
  • Fluoride Treatments: Strengthens enamel and helps resist decay.
  • Dental Sealants: Protective coatings for cavity prevention on molars.
  • Oral Hygiene Education: Coaching families on brushing, flossing, and healthy diets.
  • Digital X-rays: Safe imaging to track development and catch hidden issues.

Why Is Pediatric Preventive Dentistry Important?

  • Prevents cavities and gum disease
  • Detects developmental issues early
  • Builds good oral hygiene habits
  • Reduces dental anxiety
  • Improves long-term oral health

Frequently Asked Questions

When should my child first see a dentist?

By their first birthday or within six months of their first tooth coming in.

Are dental cleanings necessary for baby teeth?

Yes. Baby teeth are critical for speech, eating, and guiding permanent teeth.

What are dental sealants and are they safe?

Sealants are safe, quick coatings that prevent cavities in back teeth.

Does my child need fluoride treatments?

Yes, especially if they are prone to cavities. We assess this during each visit.

How often should I schedule checkups?

Every six months unless otherwise advised by your pediatric dentist.
